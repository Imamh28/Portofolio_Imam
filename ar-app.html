DIGESTIFY: AR Learning App

A gamified learning application for the digestive system using Augmented Reality and 3D Models, a finalist in the 2024 National Student Scientific Work Competition (LIDM).

Problem Statement

Student understanding of the human digestive system is often low. Data shows that 53.64% of junior high school students in Pontianak did not achieve mastery in this subject, with an average score of only 38.18 for high school students. The main causes identified were boring and less interactive learning methods.

Solution Research

Research from Virti (2023) shows that technologies like AR and VR provide tangible benefits such as improved training quality, long-term cost efficiency, and higher student engagement. The visual-interactive method proves to be more effective than conventional learning.

Agile Methodology

The development of Digestify follows the Agile framework, divided into three main phases: Pre-implementation, Implementation, and Post-implementation. This approach allows the team to adapt quickly to user feedback and continuously improve the application's quality.

Solution Determination

Digestify was developed based on a multi-method learning approach, ranging from lectures, 3D simulations, self-study, to discussions. This solution addresses the needs of students who have difficulty understanding abstract concepts and visualizing the human digestive system.

Design Process

To solve the problem of boring digestive system learning, we designed a mobile-based solution with an engaging visual approach. The initial design started from rough wireframes, then developed into a more intuitive and user-friendly digital UI prototype.

User Evaluation

Evaluation was conducted using test (formative and summative) and non-test (interview) techniques. The results showed an average validation score of >8.0 (excellent category), with teacher ratings reaching 92%. This proves that Digestify is not only well received by students but also considered effective by teaching staff.
